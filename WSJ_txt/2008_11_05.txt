The Dow Jones Industrial Average plunged 486.01 points, or 5.05%. The decline, the worst percentage loss ever for the day after a presidential election, reflected renewed economic worries on top of a series of unsustainable gains in Treasury prices.
A plan to aid ailing auto makers could be doubled to $50 billion under a second economic-stimulus plan Democrats are considering.
Treasury is exploring more ways to apply the $700 billion financial- rescue package.
Google backed out of its ad pact with Yahoo as regulators threatened to sue. The move was seen as a renewed opening for Microsoft.
U.S. borrowing in coming months will be at record levels, and could constrain Obama's economic agenda.
GMAC Financial posted a $2.52 billion loss and disclosed further trouble in home and auto lending.

Time Warner posted flat net income as AOL and Time Inc. offset better results in cable and movies.
Europe's economic pic- ture has darkened further, service-sector activity data showed, making interest-rate cuts more likely.

FairPoint is searching for a new CEO as it attempts to integrate landline assets it purchased from Verizon.

Arcelor plans to cut steel output at least 35% -- more than double what it disclosed last month.
