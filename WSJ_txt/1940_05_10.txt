Most Over Counter Sections Register Sharp Declines
Liquidating pressure was evident in virtually all sections of the over-the-counter market yesterday. Top-flight utility and industrial bonds were marked down by as much as two points, olflowing the overnight German invasion of Holland and Belgium: issues of the more speculative types followed the irregularly lower course of stock market values.
Railroad issues were relatively hard-hit. although traders reported buying interest in the defaulted group during the late afternoon. This interest was concentrated chiefly on obligations scheduled to participate in new first mortgages under reorganization. Bank stocks reflected the decline in the Treasury bond group.
